================================================================================
CAT BREED STATISTICAL ANALYSIS - EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================
Analysis Date: November 10, 2025
Dataset: 550 cats across 10 breeds

KEY FINDINGS:
----------------------------------------

1. HEALTH & PHYSIOLOGY ANALYSIS
   ───────────────────────────────────
   • Life Expectancy Range: 12.6 - 15.8 years
   • Longest-lived breed: Siamese (15.8 ± 1.6 years)
   • Shortest-lived breed: Persian (12.6 ± 1.8 years)
   • Weight Range: 8.7 - 13.0 lbs
   • Heaviest breed: Ragdoll (13.0 lbs average)
   • Lightest breed: Russian Blue (8.7 lbs average)
   • Significant gender dimorphism: Males average 3.4 lbs heavier than females
     (Males: 12.2 lbs, Females: 8.8 lbs)

   Health Condition Prevalence:
   • Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD): Overall 6.5%
     - Highest risk: Persian (43.6%)
   • Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM): Overall 5.5%
     - Highest risk: Maine Coon (10.9%)
   • Hip Dysplasia: Overall 4.0%
     - Highest risk: Maine Coon (16.4%)

2. PERSONALITY CHARACTERISTICS (QUANTIFIED)
   ──────────────────────────────────────────
   • Vocalization Frequency (1=Low, 2=Moderate, 3=High):
     - Most vocal: Siamese (2.75)
     - Least vocal: British Shorthair (1.22)
   • Social Interaction Need (1=Independent, 2=Moderate, 3=High):
     - Most social: Abyssinian (2.73)
     - Most independent: British Shorthair (1.25)
   • Affection Level (1=Aloof, 2=Moderate, 3=Lap-sitter, 4=Dog-like):
     - Most affectionate: Abyssinian (3.67)
     - Most aloof: Russian Blue (1.36)
   • Strong correlation between social need and affection level (r = 0.461)
